e v o l v e CSS

A Sass-based CSS architecture style guide
(that becomes a framework thanks to you)

evolve CSS (ECSS) is a code style guide to help develop front-ends more scalable, modular and predictable. The idea came after some time studying architecture methodology’s as BEM, SMACSS, and others. After learning some of these interesting concepts that help us in our jobs as web developers, I decided to combine them in a way that fits my taste and way of thinking (and I’m still in that process). Since nowadays we are what we share, I’m putting it online for anyone whom this could help, and also to assist people who are looking at my HTML/CSS (remember, developers are users too).

ECSS's main objective is to guide developers to create more maintainable front-ends, but the second idea is to provide a methodology that helps a developer or team of developers make their own front-end framework that fulfill their needs. This framework can be from a lightweight structure of few commonly used elements (more like a utilities library), until a heavy framework as Bootstrap/Foundation or a methodology as BEM (or both). You will see in some minutes what I am talking about.
